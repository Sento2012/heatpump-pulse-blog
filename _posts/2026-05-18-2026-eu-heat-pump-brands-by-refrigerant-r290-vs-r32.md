The strongest signal in the 2026 EPREL heat-pump catalogue is not a brand story but a refrigerant concentration story: **R32 still dominates explicitly declared listings with 13,935 models, while R290 is at 537 and R410A at 1,896**. That gap is large enough that the market remains structurally centered on legacy HFC volumes even as propane gains visibility. The full breakdown is here: [https://househeating-pulse.com/guides/2026-eu-heat-pump-brands-by-refrigerant-r290-vs-r32](https://househeating-pulse.com/guides/2026-eu-heat-pump-brands-by-refrigerant-r290-vs-r32).

Across the snapshot, Househeating Pulse counts **60,989 EPREL-listed models from 777 manufacturers**, with an average declared **SCOP of 4.55** and average declared **power of 9.3 kW**. Refrigerant choice is therefore visible at catalogue scale, but not evenly distributed across makers. Natural refrigerants remain a small slice overall: **3.27%** of the market snapshot, with R290 specifically listed as propane (GWP 0, A3) and R32 as an A2L HFC with much higher GWP.

Brand scale is also highly concentrated. **Daikin Europe N.V.** leads the catalogue with **14,668 models and 24.05% share**, followed by **Mitsubishi Electric Europe B.V.** at **5,575 models** and **Johnson Controls Hitachi** at **5,207**. But volume leadership does not automatically translate into efficiency leadership: among the top 10 manufacturers, **Bosch Thermotechnik** posts the highest average SCOP at **4.69**, ahead of **Ariston** at **4.66**, while the Hitachi-branded Johnson Controls entity sits lowest in that group at **4.18**.

The practical conclusion is narrow but useful: the registry clearly shows where the market is largest, where efficiency is strongest, and where R290 is still numerically thin. What it does **not** provide is a complete brand-by-brand refrigerant mix for every manufacturer, so any “R290 leader” claim has to be treated carefully.
