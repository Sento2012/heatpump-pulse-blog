Europe’s heat-pump market still has a clear leader, but the more important signal is the tightening race underneath it. In the latest EPREL snapshot, **Daikin Europe N.V.** holds **24.05%** of listed models, while **Mitsubishi Electric Europe B.V.** has climbed to **9.14%** and **Hitachi** sits just behind at **8.54%**. Together, the top three brands represent **41.73%** of the catalog, yet the second and third places are separated by only **0.60 percentage points**.

The scale gap remains large: Daikin lists **14,668** models versus **5,575** for Mitsubishi and **5,207** for Hitachi. But the upper tier is not settling into a fixed hierarchy. The next two brands, **Bosch Thermotechnik** at **5.91%** and **Ariston** at **4.29%**, compress the middle further, taking the top five to **51.93%** of all listed models. That concentration is meaningful, but it is not monopoly-like: the full snapshot contains **60,989** models from **777** manufacturers.

Efficiency does not map neatly onto share. Mitsubishi posts the highest average SCOP among the top three at **4.51**, ahead of Daikin’s **4.44** and Hitachi’s **4.18**. Bosch and Ariston also sit above Daikin on this metric, which suggests that scale and efficiency are not moving in lockstep.

The broader market structure helps explain where share can still move. **Air-water** units account for **30,452** listings, roughly half the catalog, while **air-air** adds **21,065** and **hp-water-heater** another **9,228**. By contrast, specialist categories such as **ground-water** and **water-water** remain tiny. Refrigerant mix shows a similar pattern: **R32** dominates with **13,935** models, while natural refrigerants remain a small share of the total.
