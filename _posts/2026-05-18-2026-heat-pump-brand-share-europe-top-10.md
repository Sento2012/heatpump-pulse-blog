# 2026 heat-pump brand share in Europe: who leads the market, and where

Europe’s 2026 heat-pump listing market is concentrated, but not close to monopolistic. In the EPREL-based sample, 60,989 models are listed, and the top 10 brands together cover 60.88% of them. That leaves a long tail of 39.12% outside the dominant group, so brand visibility is skewed toward a few large manufacturers without collapsing into a single-vendor market.

The main structural fact is Daikin’s lead. Daikin Europe N.V. accounts for 14,668 models, or 24.05% of all listed models, which is well ahead of Mitsubishi Electric Europe at 5,575 models (9.14%) and Johnson Controls Hitachi at 5,207 models (8.54%). The gap from first to second place is large enough to matter operationally: Daikin alone has more than 2.6x the listed models of Mitsubishi Electric, and nearly triple Hitachi’s count.

The middle of the leaderboard is tighter. Bosch Thermotechnik sits fourth with 5.91%, followed by Ariston at 4.29%, then a compressed band from Atlantic at 2.49% down to Panasonic at 1.47%. That shape points to a market with a clear leader and several credible contenders rather than a winner-take-all structure.

Performance does not track share in a simple way. Bosch has the highest average SCOP among the top 10 at 4.69, above Daikin’s 4.44 and above the top-three average of 4.38. The average of ranks 4–10 is 4.48, slightly higher than the top three, which is a useful reminder that listing scale and efficiency rankings are related but not interchangeable.

Country and technology splits likely matter, but the registry data here does not expose brand share by country or by heat-pump type. What it does show is that air-to-water dominates the catalog at 30,452 models, while R32 is the leading refrigerant declaration by a wide margin.
