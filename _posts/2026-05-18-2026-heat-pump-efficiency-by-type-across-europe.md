The efficiency gap across Europe’s heat-pump categories is real, but the market structure matters as much as the thermodynamics. In the current EPREL sample, **air-to-water systems dominate the catalog with 30,452 models, or 49.9% of all listings**, while **air-to-air accounts for 21,065 models (34.5%)**. Ground-source options are much smaller pools: **ground-water has 213 models** and **water-water only 31**. That distribution shapes what buyers can actually compare, not just which technology looks best on paper.

On reported seasonal performance, the ranking is clear among the types where SCOP is available: **water-water leads at 6.15**, **ground-water follows at 4.77**, and **air-water averages 4.54**. Put differently, the best recorded type is **1.61 SCOP points, or 35.5%, above air-water**. Ground-water still holds a modest edge over air-water at **0.23 points**. The canonical breakdown is here: [2026 heat-pump efficiency by type across Europe](https://househeating-pulse.com/guides/2026-heat-pump-efficiency-by-type-across-europe).

The catch is scale. The highest-average category, water-water, is also the rarest serious segment in the dataset, so its lead does not translate into broad product availability. That distinction shows up again in the model-level leaderboard: **11 of the top 15 SCOP entries are air-water**, and **4 are water-water**. So the biggest category is not the best on average, but it is where most of the market’s standout units still appear.

There are also non-efficiency trade-offs. Average outdoor noise is **42.0 dB for water-water**, versus **58.8 dB for ground-water**, **59.8 dB for air-water**, and **64.1 dB for air-air**. Average capacity also rises sharply in the ground-source segment, with **35.65 kW for water-water** and **18.45 kW for ground-water**, compared with **11.83 kW for air-water**.
