The 2026 EPREL snapshot shows a market where scale and efficiency only partially overlap. Among the top 20 brands by listing count, LG Electronics Deutschland GmbH leads on average SCOP at **4.93** across **405 models**, while STIEBEL ELTRON follows at **4.84** across **433**. That efficiency edge is real, but it is not backed by the largest catalogs: Daikin still dominates the registry with **14,668 listings** and a **24.05%** share, yet its average SCOP is **4.44**, below the full-market mean of **4.55**.

The most important pattern is that some brands combine both breadth and performance. Bosch Thermotechnik GmbH is the clearest case: **3,602 models**, **5.91%** of listings, and an average SCOP of **4.69**. Ariston SpA is similar at **2,618 models** and **4.66** SCOP. For buyers, that matters more than a narrow specialist range, because it suggests the efficiency signal survives at commercial scale rather than appearing only in small, curated lineups.

The spread at the top is also tighter than many would expect. The gap between the best and fifth-best brand in the top-20 efficiency ranking is only **0.27 SCOP points** (from **4.93** to **4.66**), which means the leading group is clustered rather than split into distinct classes. At the other end of the same cohort, Toshiba Carrier Europe S.A.S averages **3.93**, a full **1.00 point** behind LG.

For the broader market context, the live catalog still skews heavily toward **air-water** systems (**30,452 models**) and **R32** refrigerant listings (**13,935**), while **R290** remains relatively small at **537 models**. The brand-level extract here does not support precise refrigerant or price-band shares, so the strongest conclusion is structural: catalog depth, efficiency, and product strategy do not align neatly.
