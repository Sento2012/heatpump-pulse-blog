Austria’s heat-pump market looks broad at first glance, but the underlying structure is concentrated and still heavily air-source. EPREL currently lists **60,989 models from 777 manufacturers** in Austria, yet the top five brands already control **51.93%** of all listings, with **Daikin alone at 24.05%**. That combination matters: buyers face a long tail of brands, but most of the practical shortlist comes from a small set of registrants.

The second key pattern is technology mix. Nearly **50%** of Austrian listings are **air-water** units, while **air-air** adds another **34.54%**. Ground-source formats remain niche at **0.35%** for ground-water and **0.05%** for water-water. In efficiency terms, the market is stacked toward the top end: **APPP accounts for 38.48%** of listings, and the top three classes (**APPP, APP, AP**) cover **80.73%**. That suggests a registry dominated by high-declared-performance products, even if real-world outcomes still depend on system design and operating conditions.

Refrigerant data points to a different kind of inertia. **R32** is still the largest visible family at **13,935 listings** (**22.85%**), while **R290** reaches only **537 models** (**0.88%**). Natural refrigerants overall are just **3.27%** of the Austrian catalog, so the transition away from higher-GWP options is visible, but far from complete.

The economics side is more supportive than in some nearby markets. Austria’s household electricity-to-gas price ratio is **2.68**, below Germany’s **3.16** and below the rough **3.7** break-even reference often used for heat-pump comparisons. That does not guarantee savings, but it improves the case relative to more gas-favourable markets.
