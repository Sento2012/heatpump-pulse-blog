The sharpest finding is negative: Europe’s EPREL snapshot does **not** yet support a defensible brand-by-brand ranking of who “won” the R290 transition in 2026. What it does show is a market still dominated by a few large manufacturers, while propane adoption remains small relative to R32.

Across the catalog, manufacturer totals sum to **60,989 models**. Daikin Europe N.V. leads with **14,668 models (24.05%)**, followed by Mitsubishi Electric Europe B.V. at **5,575 (9.14%)** and Johnson Controls Hitachi Air Conditioning Europe at **5,207 (8.54%)**. Bosch Thermotechnik GmbH sits on **3,602 (5.91%)**, ahead of Ariston SpA at **2,618 (4.29%)**. That concentration matters because even a fast refrigerant transition can look broad in aggregate while still being highly uneven by supplier.

On refrigerants, the imbalance is even clearer. The declared universe contains **537 R290 entries** versus **13,935 R32 entries** and **1,896 R410A entries**. Even adding minor case-variant propane labels barely changes the picture. In other words, R290 is visible, but it is not yet the dominant choice across the European catalog. The available data also show R290 with **GWP 0**, while R32 remains on the regulatory clock with a **2027-01-01** phase-out date in the reference table.

The analytical limit is important: the corpus does **not** include a prior manufacturer-level refrigerant snapshot, so there is no way to calculate share change, identify the fastest mover, or measure the gap between the first and second brand.
