Daikin’s Europe-wide lead is substantial, but it is not a clean proxy for local market control. Across **60,989 EPREL-listed heat-pump models**, Daikin accounts for **14,668 models** and **24.05%** share, far ahead of Mitsubishi Electric at **9.14%** and Johnson Controls Hitachi at **8.54%**. Yet the market is not winner-take-all: the **top 3 brands hold 41.73%**, the **top 5 only 51.93%**, and the **top 10 just 60.88%** of the catalog, leaving a long tail of **777 manufacturers** to fill the rest.

That structure matters because product mix and national conditions can reshape the hierarchy. Europe’s EPREL stock is dominated by **air-water** and **air-air** units — **30,452** and **21,065** models respectively — while **heat-pump water heaters** add another **9,228**. In other words, a brand with strength in one segment can look disproportionately large or small depending on the country’s demand profile.

The country backdrop is just as uneven. France has the lowest electricity-to-gas ratio among the five large markets at **1.78**, while Poland sits at **3.71**; heating degree days range from **1,536.47** in Italy to **3,706.42** in Poland. Carbon intensity also spans a wide band, from **56 gCO₂/kWh** in France to **661 gCO₂/kWh** in Poland. Those spreads help explain why a Europe-wide leader does not automatically dominate every national market.

The deeper point is methodological: aggregate brand share is a useful scale indicator, not a shortcut to country-level leadership.
