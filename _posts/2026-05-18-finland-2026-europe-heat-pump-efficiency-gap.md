Finland’s 2026 heat-pump signal is not scale, but **selection**: a market tilted toward the most efficient cold-climate product classes would sit above Europe’s already respectable **4.55 SCOP** baseline. The widest structural premium is in **water-water** systems, which average **6.15 SCOP**, followed by **ground-water** at **4.77**; by comparison, the dominant European category, **air-water**, is only **4.54**. That spread is the core of Finland’s efficiency gap.

The European reference market is broad — **60,989 models** from **777 manufacturers** — but it is also highly concentrated in one category: **30,452 air-water listings** versus just **213 ground-water** and **31 water-water** models. In other words, any country with a colder-climate mix naturally pulls away from the continent’s average, even without a larger catalog footprint.

Finland’s climate reinforces that pattern. The country profile shows **4,407.92 heating degree days**, an annual mean temperature of **6.37°C**, and a January mean of **-2.92°C**. That makes the efficiency of hydronic, ground-coupled, and water-coupled systems materially more relevant than in milder European markets.

The same logic applies on refrigerants and brands. Europe is still dominated by **R32** with **13,935 listings**, while **R290** remains small at **537**. On the manufacturer side, high-volume efficiency is not evenly distributed: **Bosch Thermotechnik** averages **4.69 SCOP** and **Ariston** **4.66**, while **Daikin Europe** sits lower at **4.44** across a much larger catalog. The data points to a market where performance and scale do not move together.

Finland-specific listing shares are not available in this dataset, so the exact uplift cannot be calculated.
