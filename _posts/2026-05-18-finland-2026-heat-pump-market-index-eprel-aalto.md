Finland is best read as a **cold-climate stress test** for the European heat-pump catalog, not as a separate EPREL universe. The registry itself is broad — **60,989 models from 777 manufacturers** — but Finland’s operating context is defined by **4,407.92 heating degree days**, a **-2.92°C average January temperature**, and a low-carbon grid at **79.0 g/kWh**. That combination makes the country useful for checking whether the market’s published performance is actually aligned with heating-led demand.

What the dataset can show clearly is the structure of the wider EU supply base Finnish buyers are drawing from. The catalog is concentrated at the top: **Daikin Europe N.V.** lists **14,668 models** and **24.05%** of the total, while **Mitsubishi Electric Europe B.V.** has **5,575 models** (**9.14%**) and **Johnson Controls Hitachi** **5,207** (**8.54%**). Together, those three account for **41.73%** of all EPREL listings. Scale does not map cleanly to efficiency, though: Bosch’s average **SCOP of 4.69** is higher than Daikin’s **4.44**, while Hitachi sits below the overall catalog average of **4.55** at **4.18**.

Type mix is even more informative. **Air-water** dominates with **30,452 models** — almost **50%** of the registry — and posts an average **SCOP of 4.54**. **Ground-water** is smaller at **213 models** but improves to **4.77**, while **water-water** is rare at just **31 listings** yet leads on paper with **SCOP 6.15** and **42.0 dB** average outdoor noise. The live market data on this angle is available in our full piece: [Finland 2026 heat-pump market index](https://househeating-pulse.com/guides/finland-2026-heat-pump-market-index-eprel-aalto).

The practical limitation is that EPREL does **not** expose a Finland-only retail subset, so country-specific brand share and type concentration cannot be proven from the registry alone.
