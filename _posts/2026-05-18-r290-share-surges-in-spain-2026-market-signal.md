Spain’s 2026 heat-pump listing set is signaling a refrigerant transition, not just a larger catalog. **R290 now holds 53.7% of listings, ahead of R32 at 43.8%**, a **9.9-point gap** that makes propane the leading option in the Spanish EPREL-linked universe rather than a specialist niche.

The raw counts show how decisive that shift is: out of **1,281 total listings**, **688 are R290** and **561 are R32**. Only **32 listings** use other refrigerants combined. In other words, Spain’s market is already clustering around two refrigerant families, with the low-GWP path now on top. That is a meaningful manufacturer signal because listing data reflects what suppliers are actively registering and promoting, not just what buyers happen to ask for.

The context matters. Spain’s electricity price sits at **€0.2669/kWh**, while gas is **€0.0955/kWh**, and qualifying installations can receive up to **€3,000** or **80% of cost** under RD 1124/2021. In a market shaped by both operating economics and subsidy design, refrigerant choice becomes a compliance-and-portfolio decision, not an abstract technical detail. Spain’s climate profile — **2,252 heating degree days** and **623 cooling degree days** — also makes the product mix relevant for both heating and reversible systems.

Against the EU-wide listing base, Spain looks extreme: the broader market still shows **R290 at just 0.9%** of listings and **R32 at 22.8%**. That makes Spain’s R290 share more than fifty percentage points above the European average in the current snapshot.
