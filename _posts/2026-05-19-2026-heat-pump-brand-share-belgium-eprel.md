Belgium’s 2026 EPREL heat-pump market looks broad on paper, but the live catalog is structurally concentrated: the top 15 manufacturers account for 39,846 of 60,989 listed models, or 65.33% of the visible pool. That means the market is not evenly distributed across brands, and the leading names matter disproportionately for both product availability and technical direction.

At the top of the Belgian-facing ranking, Daikin Europe N.V. holds 14,668 models and 24.05% share, well ahead of Mitsubishi Electric Europe B.V. at 5,575 models (9.14%) and Johnson Controls Hitachi at 5,207 (8.54%). Bosch Thermotechnik and Ariston follow with 5.91% and 4.29% respectively. The top five alone represent 51.93% of listings, which is a high degree of front-end concentration even before the long tail is considered.

The more interesting split is not just share, but performance. Daikin’s average SCOP is 4.44, while Bosch reaches 4.69 and Ariston 4.66. In other words, the biggest catalog presence is not the highest-efficiency portfolio. For Belgian buyers facing electricity at €0.3499/kWh and gas at €0.0898/kWh, those differences in seasonal efficiency can materially change operating cost assumptions. The tariff ratio is close enough to the break-even zone that SCOP gaps of a few tenths still matter.

Refrigerants tell a second structural story. EPREL declarations in the supplied corpus are dominated by R32 at 13,935 entries, versus 537 for R290 and 1,955 for the R410 family when spelling variants are grouped. That does not measure sales, but it does show where the installed-product development base still sits: heavily toward R32, with propane still a much smaller share of the listing universe.
