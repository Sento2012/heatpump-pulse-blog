EPREL’s 2026 snapshot shows a market that is broad on paper but highly concentrated in practice: **60,989 listed heat-pump models from 777 manufacturers**, with the **top 5 brands accounting for 51.93%** of all listings and the **top 10 reaching 60.88%**. That concentration matters more once refrigerants are separated, because the visible product pool is not evenly distributed between **R32** and **R290**.

The raw refrigerant split is already skewed. EPREL currently records **13,935 R32 models** versus only **537 R290 models**. In other words, R290 makes up **0.88%** of all listed models, while R32 alone represents **22.85%**. That gap is large enough to distort any “market share” discussion unless the analysis is broken down by refrigerant and not just by brand.

At the manufacturer level, the all-market leaderboard is led by **Daikin Europe N.V.** with **14,668 listings** and **24.05% share** of the EPREL model universe. **Mitsubishi Electric Europe B.V.** follows at **9.14%**, and **Johnson Controls Hitachi** at **8.54%**. Together, the top three control **41.73%** of all listed models. The concentration is real, but it is a portfolio concentration, not a sales chart.

Why the refrigerant split matters is structural: **R290 has GWP 0**, while **R32 is listed at GWP 771** and carries a phase-out date in the cited schedule. That means the segment question is not just “which brand is biggest?” but “which brands are positioning their portfolios around the refrigerant likely to survive longer under policy pressure?”
