## Czechia’s refrigerant transition is still early-stage

Czechia’s 2026 heat-pump listing picture is still defined far more by **R32** than by **R290**. In the current market snapshot, only **3.27%** of listed models use R290, while **84.47%** still use R32. That gap is large enough to say the market has not yet crossed into a low-GWP default, even if R290 is now visible in the catalog.

The broader index helps explain why. Across **60,989** listed models, there are just **537 R290** entries versus **13,935 R32** units. In other words, the new refrigerant is present, but it remains a small minority of the available model pool. For analysts tracking product availability rather than installed base, that is a strong signal that Czechia is still in the transition phase, not the endpoint.

A useful way to read this is against the region’s operating context. Czechia’s household electricity price is **€0.3217/kWh** and gas is **€0.0961/kWh**, a ratio of **3.35x**. That sits in the middle of the nearby country range, so the refrigerant mix is not being explained by an obvious outlier in energy pricing alone. Support is also present: the two main Czech subsidy programmes both cap at **€4,900**, with **NZÚ Light** covering up to **100%** and **NZÚ Standard** up to **50%** of project cost.
