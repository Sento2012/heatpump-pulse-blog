The sharpest signal in Europe’s 2026 heat-pump registry is not just who leads overall, but how little refrigerant choice is shaping the market compared with brand depth and unit type. EPREL-backed data in the Househeating Pulse market snapshot covers **60,989 models from 777 manufacturers**, yet **R32 appears 13,935 times** while **R290 shows only 537 entries**. That means the “next-gen refrigerant” story is still a small slice of the actual catalog, even as policy pressure tightens around higher-GWP gases.

The market is also structurally concentrated by type. **Air-water accounts for 30,452 models**, far ahead of **air-air at 21,065** and **hp-water-heater at 9,228**; ground-water and water-water remain niche at **213** and **31** models respectively. For most buyers and installers, that means the practical competition is happening inside a few large segments, not evenly across the full registry. The live catalog and filters make that visible in detail: https://househeating-pulse.com/guides/2026-heat-pump-brand-share-in-europe-by-refrigerant-and-type

At the brand level, the hierarchy is similarly top-heavy. **Daikin Europe N.V. leads with 14,668 models and 24.05% share**, followed by **Mitsubishi Electric Europe B.V.** at **5,575 models / 9.14%**, and **Johnson Controls Hitachi** at **5,207 / 8.54%**. The top five brands together hold **51.93%** of listed models, and the top ten reach **60.84%**. That is a strong indication that market access is defined by a narrow leading group, with a long tail behind it.

Efficiency leadership is not the same as scale leadership. **LG Electronics Deutschland GmbH** posts the highest average SCOP among the ranked brands at **4.93**, while **Stiebel Eltron** follows at **4.84**. Meanwhile, Daikin’s average SCOP is **4.44**, slightly below Ariston’s **4.66**, despite Daikin’s much larger footprint.
