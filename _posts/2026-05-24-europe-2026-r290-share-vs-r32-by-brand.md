## Europe’s refrigerant shift is real — but brand exposure is still uneven

The important signal in Europe’s 2026 heat-pump market is not just that **R290 is now the strategic endpoint**; it’s that the transition is landing unevenly across large catalogues. In the EPREL snapshot, the supplied refrigerant table lists **537 R290 entries** against **13,935 R32 entries**, while the full heat-pump universe totals **60,989 models**. That gap means the market-wide split cannot be computed cleanly from this corpus, but the direction of travel is still clear: legacy R32 remains deeply embedded.

That matters most for the biggest portfolios. **Daikin Europe N.V.** leads the EPREL listing with **14,668 models** — **24.05%** of all listed units — followed by **Mitsubishi Electric Europe B.V.** at **5,575** models (**9.14%**) and **Hitachi** at **5,207** (**8.54%**). Together, the top five manufacturers account for **31,670 models**, or **51.93%** of the total. In other words, even a modest refrigerant shift inside one of these catalogues moves far more of the market than a niche player’s full portfolio.

The structural reason the refrigerant mix is now a portfolio metric is straightforward: in the supplied reference table, **R32 carries a GWP of 771** and an EU phase-out date of **2027-01-01**, while **R290 is listed with GWP 0**. That makes refrigerant choice more than a technical detail; it is a proxy for roadmap timing, compliance risk, and installer readiness.

The catch is evidence quality. This dataset does **not** include a manufacturer-by-refrigerant cross-tab, so it cannot rank the worst R32 laggards or the clearest R290 leaders without speculation. Still, the catalogue scale is enough to show where the strategic risk sits: large incumbents with broad, mixed portfolios will determine the pace of transition more than small specialists.
