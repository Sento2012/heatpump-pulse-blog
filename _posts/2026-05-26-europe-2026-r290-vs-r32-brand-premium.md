R290 is gaining strategic importance in Europe’s heat-pump catalog, but the data still shows a market that is structurally R32-led rather than propane-led. In the current listing universe, EPREL-derived counts show **13,935 declared R32 entries versus 537 for R290** — roughly **25.95 times** more R32 models. That makes propane visible, but not dominant, despite the policy narrative pushing in that direction.

The broader index reinforces the same point. Househeating Pulse’s 2026 snapshot tracks **60,989 heat-pump models across 777 manufacturers**, with natural refrigerants making up only **3.27%** of the catalog. In other words, refrigerant choice is becoming a commercial signal before it becomes a volume majority. The gap matters because manufacturers can already use R290 to position products as future-facing or compliance-aligned, even while the mainstream catalog remains centered on legacy HFCs and transitional blends.

Brand concentration makes that signal more consequential. The top five manufacturers account for **51.93%** of all indexed models, led by **Daikin Europe N.V.** at **14,668 models** and **24.05%** share, followed by **Mitsubishi Electric Europe B.V.** at **5,575 models** and **9.14%**. When a small number of vendors controls that much of the catalog, a pricing move on R290 can influence market perception quickly — especially if it is presented as a premium feature rather than a simple refrigerant swap.
