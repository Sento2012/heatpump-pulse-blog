The clearest signal in this EPREL-based index is that Europe’s smaller EU-15 heat-pump markets are likely to diverge by product structure, not just by size. Across the full catalogue, the average listed model posts a SCOP of 4.55, but the segment mix is highly uneven: water-to-water systems average 6.15 SCOP across just 31 models, while air-to-water — the market’s centre of gravity — sits at 4.54 across 30,452 models. That gap matters because smaller markets with a heavier specialist share can look structurally more efficient than those dominated by mainstream split and monobloc lines.

The same pattern appears in labels and refrigerants. A+++ accounts for 23,466 of 60,989 listed models, or about 38.5%, suggesting a catalogue that is already heavily tilted toward the upper label bands. But the refrigerant transition is still incomplete: R32 is listed 13,935 times, R410A 1,896 times, and R290 only 537 times. On the market-index measure, natural refrigerants still make up just 3.27% of the catalogue. The policy direction is clear, but the EPREL snapshot shows an industry that is moving in layers rather than flipping wholesale.

Brand concentration is equally important for reading the smaller markets. Europe-wide, Daikin alone holds 14,668 listings, or 24.05% of the catalogue, far ahead of Mitsubishi Electric on 9.14% and Hitachi on 8.54%. That concentration implies that some national catalogues may be defined by a few multinational suppliers, while others may leave room for more fragmented, specialist offerings.
