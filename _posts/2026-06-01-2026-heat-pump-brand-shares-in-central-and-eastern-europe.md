The strongest signal in this CEE brief is not country-by-country brand dominance, but how much the regional market is shaped by a few structural variables: a concentrated EU supplier base, a product mix dominated by air-water units, and sharply different running-cost economics across smaller markets. On the available EPREL snapshot, the EU catalog contains **60,989 models from 777 manufacturers**, yet the top brand, **Daikin**, still holds **24.05%** of listings. The top three brands together reach **47.73%**, which is the benchmark to keep in mind when assessing competitive pressure in Central and Eastern Europe.

That concentration matters because the region is not one uniform demand block. The available country-level economics show a clear split: **Bulgaria (2.09)**, **Slovakia (3.05)**, **Czechia (3.35)**, and **Hungary (3.23)** all sit below the approximate **3.7 electricity-to-gas ratio** where a SCOP 4 heat pump starts to look attractive on operating cost alone. **Poland is essentially at parity at 3.71**, while **Romania is well above it at 5.11**. In other words, the same product and brand portfolio will face very different payback conditions depending on the market.

The product architecture is also clear. **Air-water heat pumps make up 30,452 models, or 49.93%** of the catalog, far ahead of air-air units at 21,065. That points to where volume and installer relevance sit in the broader European market, including CEE.

Refrigerants show a similar imbalance. **R32 appears in 13,935 models (22.85%)**, while **R290 appears in just 537 (0.88%)**. Natural refrigerants are rising in policy relevance, but they are still a small share of declared listings.
