The clearest signal in the 2026 EPREL heat-pump snapshot is not a refrigerant trendline but a concentration problem: a small number of manufacturers and one declared refrigerant category dominate the visible market. Out of 60,989 listed models from 777 manufacturers, **R32 alone appears in 13,935 entries**, while **R290 shows up in just 537**. That makes R32 roughly **26x larger** than propane in the explicitly declared slice of the registry, even before you look at brand structure.

That matters because the catalog is not evenly distributed. **Daikin Europe N.V.** leads the full listing count with **14,668 models (24.05%)**, followed by **Mitsubishi Electric Europe B.V.** at **5,575 (9.14%)** and **Johnson Controls Hitachi** at **5,207 (8.54%)**. The top five brands together hold **51.93%** of all listings, so buyer choice is already shaped by a narrow OEM set rather than a broad long tail.

Efficiency does not track perfectly with scale. The market-average SCOP is **4.55**, but Daikin sits slightly below that at **4.44**, while **Bosch Thermotechnik** is above at **4.69** and **LG** reaches **4.93** on a much smaller catalog. That spread is a reminder that brand size, refrigerant choice, and performance should be treated as separate filters, not assumed to move together.

The refrigerant side is also messy at the registry level. R410A and R410a are split across labels, and the data here does **not** support a defensible brand-by-refrigerant ranking for R290 or R32 specifically.
