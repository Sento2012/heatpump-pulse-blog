The European heat-pump brand hierarchy is still concentrating, but the ordering at the top is not stable. In the latest EPREL snapshot, Daikin Europe N.V. leads with **14,668 listed models**, while **Mitsubishi Electric Europe B.V.** has **5,575** and **Johnson Controls Hitachi Air Conditioning Europe** has **5,207**. That places Daikin at **24.05%** share, with second and third separated by only **0.60 percentage points** — a narrow gap in a market that now contains **60,989 models from 777 manufacturers**.

That concentration is real, but not absolute. The top three account for **41.73%** of all listed models, and it takes **five manufacturers** to clear the 50% mark. By the time the list reaches the top 15 brands, coverage rises to **65.33%** of the market. The implication is straightforward: a few large portfolios still dominate visibility, yet the leaderboard remains competitive below the first spot.

Efficiency does not map cleanly onto share. Mitsubishi Electric posts the best average SCOP among the top three at **4.51**, ahead of Daikin’s **4.44** and Johnson Controls Hitachi’s **4.18**. But the overall market average is **4.55**, so all three trail the broader benchmark. Bosch, ranked fourth by model count, actually reports a higher average SCOP at **4.69**. The share ranking is therefore not a proxy for the best seasonal performance.

The deeper structural shift is refrigerant strategy. Across the full market, **R290** accounts for just **537 declared models**, versus **13,935** for **R32**. That leaves propane at less than **1%** of all listed models, even as policy pressure builds around legacy refrigerants. The market is early in this migration, but brand-level positioning is likely to change as manufacturers expand their R290 catalogues.
