R290 has moved from edge case to market default in Europe’s 2026 heat-pump catalog. In the latest EPREL snapshot, propane-coded listings make up **53.7%** of the combined R290/R32 pool, with **16,478** R290 entries versus **13,935** for R32. The gap is not symbolic: R290 leads by **2,543 listings**, which is enough to call this a structural shift rather than a temporary spike.

That matters because the underlying catalog is now too large for this to be a single-brand anomaly. EPREL shows **60,989** heat-pump models from **777 manufacturers**, so the refrigerant mix is being set across a broad European supply base. On the Househeating Pulse [market snapshot](https://househeating-pulse.com/articles/europe-2026-r290-share-crosses-the-majority-mark), R290 is no longer best described as an emerging option — it is the code buyers are increasingly likely to see first when comparing new hydronic products.

The brand structure reinforces that point. A small number of manufacturers still anchor the catalog: **Daikin Europe N.V.** alone accounts for **14,668** models, or **24.05%** of all listings, while **Mitsubishi Electric Europe B.V.** has **5,575** and **Bosch Thermotechnik GmbH** **3,602**. When the largest portfolios shift refrigerant direction, the market-wide share can move quickly.

What this snapshot does **not** yet prove is a pricing or efficiency advantage. The dataset does not provide a clean R290-vs-R32 price comparison, nor refrigerant-specific SCOP averages. It also does not show whether R290 dominates the top-efficiency leaderboard. So the strongest conclusion is narrower: **portfolio strategy has already turned, even if the economics have not yet been fully quantified**.
