The most useful signal in this comparison is not a country champion, but a structural one: the EPREL heat-pump catalog is already highly concentrated. Across the indexed universe there are **60,989 models** from **777 manufacturers**, and the top five brands alone account for **51.93%** of all listings. Daikin leads with **24.05%** of listings, followed by Mitsubishi Electric at **9.14%** and Hitachi at **8.54%**. That concentration sets the context for Austria, Sweden and Finland, even though the supplied registry extract does not expose country-level brand-share tables for those three markets.

The same limitation applies to refrigerants and technology mix by country. At market level, though, the picture is clear: **R32** appears on **13,935** listings, while **R290** is present on just **537**. Natural refrigerants make up only **3.27%** of the indexed market, so the transition toward propane is visible but still early. In product architecture, **air-water units dominate with 30,452 models**, far ahead of air-air systems at **21,065**. That means most brand competition in Europe still plays out in a large, air-water, R32-heavy segment.

Efficiency splits the leaders more than the headline shares suggest. The indexed market average SCOP is **4.55**. Daikin sits slightly below that at **4.44**, Mitsubishi is close to average at **4.51**, while Bosch and Ariston are above it at **4.69** and **4.66** respectively. So the largest catalog owner is not automatically the most efficient portfolio on paper.

For the country economics, the strongest running-cost case is Sweden: electricity is **€0.2711/kWh** and gas **€0.2092/kWh**, a ratio of **1.30**. Austria’s ratio is much steeper at **2.68**, though it also has a subsidy ceiling of **€23,000**. Finland has the coldest climate in the trio at **4,407.92 HDD 18°C**, but the corpus does not include a gas price there.
