Europe’s 2026 EPREL heat-pump leaderboard is still concentrated at the top, but the composition of the podium has changed again. Daikin remains the clear leader with **14,668 listed models**, while **Mitsubishi Electric** has moved into second place at **5,575** and **Johnson Controls Hitachi** now sits third at **5,207**. Together, those three brands account for **41.73% of all 60,989 listed models** — a strong signal that scale is still being captured by a small group of manufacturers.

The more interesting part is the structure behind that ranking. Daikin’s lead is not marginal; it is more than **9,000 models** ahead of either Mitsubishi Electric or Johnson Controls Hitachi. By contrast, the gap between second and third is only **368 models**, which means the fight below the leader is much more contestable than the fight for first. The next brands in line, **Bosch Thermotechnik** and **Ariston**, are well behind at **3,602** and **2,618** models respectively.

Efficiency does not map cleanly onto rank. Mitsubishi Electric has the strongest average SCOP of the top three at **4.51**, ahead of Daikin at **4.44** and Johnson Controls Hitachi at **4.18**. But all three remain below the EU-wide average SCOP of **4.55**. That means the brands with the largest listing footprints are not necessarily the most efficient on average, and lower-ranked manufacturers can still outperform them on seasonal efficiency.
