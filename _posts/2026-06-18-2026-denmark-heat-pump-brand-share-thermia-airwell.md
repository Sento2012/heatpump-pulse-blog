Denmark is one of the clearest examples of why the European heat-pump market cannot be read from EU-wide brand tables alone. In the EPREL corpus used for the full analysis, there is **no Denmark-level brand extract**, so the headline questions — whether **Thermia** beats **Airwell**, by how much, and how concentrated Denmark’s top five really are — cannot be answered directly from the supplied country data. That absence is itself the signal: the market may be locally distinctive, but the evidence base here does not yet quantify it.

What the dataset *does* show is the continental benchmark Denmark is being compared against. Across EPREL, there are **60,989 listed models from 777 manufacturers**, a fragmented supplier base overall. Yet the leaderboard is still top-heavy: **Daikin Europe N.V.** leads with **14,668 models** and **24.05% share**, ahead of **Mitsubishi Electric Europe B.V.** on **5,575 models** and **9.14%**. That is a gap of **9,093 models** and **14.91 percentage points**. If Denmark’s brand structure really elevates smaller names like Thermia or Airwell, it would be a meaningful deviation from the EU pattern. The same limitation applies to segment and refrigerant splits. Denmark-specific type shares are missing, but the EU baseline is clear: **air-water** units account for **30,452 models** (**49.93%**), followed by **air-air** at **21,065** (**34.54%**). On refrigerants, **R32** is the dominant declared gas with **13,935 listings** (**22.85%**), far ahead of **R410A** at **1,896** (**3.11%**).

For readers tracking national market structure, the key takeaway is simple: Europe’s heat-pump market is broad in manufacturer count, but still concentrated at the top.
