R290 has reached a clear inflection point in Europe’s listed heat-pump market: it now represents **53.7% of identifiable EPREL refrigerant entries**, ahead of **R32 at 43.5%**. That is a **10.2 percentage-point lead** inside the declared refrigerant pool, even though R290 still accounts for just **537 models out of 60,989 total EPREL listings**. The key takeaway is structural, not symbolic: propane is now the dominant declared refrigerant where EPREL exposes refrigerant data, but it is still a small share of the full listing universe.

That shift lines up with the policy and technology backdrop. R290 carries **GWP 0** and is classified **A3**, while R32 sits at **GWP 771** and R410A at **1,924**. The phase-out schedule in the refrigerant reference also places R410A and R32 under increasing pressure, which helps explain why manufacturers are moving portfolios toward propane.

The data does not give a refrigerant-by-product-type split, so it is not possible to prove which segment leads R290 adoption. But the largest listing category in EPREL is **air-water heat pumps at 30,452 models**, roughly half the universe, so that segment is the most plausible source of the visible shift in absolute terms. By contrast, **air-air** accounts for **21,065** models and **heat-pump water heaters** for **9,228**.

For buyers, the important nuance is that refrigerant choice does not map cleanly to performance. Across the full EPREL universe, average **SCOP is 4.55**, average declared power is **9.3 kW**, and average outdoor noise is **61.3 dB**. The market is moving toward propane, but model-level screening still matters more than refrigerant label alone.
