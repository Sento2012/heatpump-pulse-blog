Ukraine’s 2026 heat-pump position is defined less by performance than by absence: the EPREL-style corpus returns no `UA` country profile at all, so Ukraine cannot be ranked against peer emerging markets on model count, efficiency, tariffs, or subsidy depth. That makes the key signal structural. In this dataset, Ukraine is still outside the measurable European pack, not merely a small participant inside it.

The contrast with the Europe-wide reference frame is sharp. The market index covers **60,989 listed models** from **777 manufacturers**, with an average **SCOP of 4.55**, average **capacity of 9.3 kW**, and average **outdoor noise of 61.3 dB**. On the supply side, the continental catalogue is led by **air-water units (30,452 models)** and **air-air units (21,065)**, while **Daikin Europe N.V.** alone accounts for **14,668 models** or **24.05%** of the indexed catalogue. Those are the benchmark scales Ukraine would need to enter before a country-level comparison becomes meaningful.

The same gap appears in policy and pricing data. Ukraine has no reported electricity tariff, gas tariff, grid CO₂ intensity, or subsidy ceiling in the country table, so the usual running-cost logic — including the approximate electricity-to-gas break-even test used for SCOP 4 systems — cannot be completed here. For context, the broader dashboard spans electricity prices from **€0.1082/kWh in Hungary** to **€0.4042/kWh in Ireland**, and maximum listed subsidies from none to **€31,000 in Poland** and **€23,000 in Austria**.

The most defensible conclusion is therefore negative but precise: Ukraine is not yet visible as an emerging heat-pump market in the 2026 snapshot.
