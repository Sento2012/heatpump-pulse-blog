## 2026 EPREL brand-share: concentration is still high, but the mid-table is where movement will matter

The clearest signal from the 2026 EPREL snapshot is not a dramatic reshuffle at the top — it is how much of the market is still controlled by a small group of brands. Daikin Europe N.V. alone holds **24.05%** of listed models, with **Mitsubishi Electric Europe B.V.** at **9.14%** and **Johnson Controls Hitachi** at **8.54%**. Together, those three account for **41.73%** of the database, even though EPREL now spans **60,989 models** from **777 manufacturers**.

That concentration explains why small changes in catalogue mix can have outsized effects on brand rankings. The top five brands — Daikin, Mitsubishi Electric, Johnson Controls Hitachi, Bosch Thermotechnik and Ariston — represent **31,670 models**, or **51.93%** of all listings. In other words, the market is structurally long-tailed, but the visible share is still dominated by a narrow leading tier.

The full analysis on [househeating-pulse.com](https://househeating-pulse.com/articles/2026-eprel-brand-share-europe-top-risers) looks at what that means for ranking shifts in 2026, including the difficulty of separating true share gains from simple catalogue expansion. It also shows where the competitive pressure is likely to sit: not only among the incumbents at the top, but in the tightly packed second layer where Bosch sits at **5.91%** and Ariston at **4.29%**, followed by a cluster of brands between roughly **1.5% and 2.5%**.

The segment mix reinforces the point. **Air-to-water** units make up **30,452 models** — about half of EPREL — while air-to-air contributes **21,065** and heat-pump water heaters **9,228**. Any manufacturer with heavy exposure to the biggest segment has more room to scale its share quickly than a specialist concentrated in niche categories.
