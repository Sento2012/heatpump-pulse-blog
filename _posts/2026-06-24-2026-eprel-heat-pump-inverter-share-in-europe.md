The key finding is negative: EPREL, as used in this dataset, does **not** expose a usable inverter-control flag. That means there is no defensible way to calculate a Europe-wide inverter share, rank countries by penetration, or compare brands on inverter adoption without adding a separate tagging method. The broader catalogue is large — **60,989 models** from **777 manufacturers** in the 2026-06-24 snapshot — but inverter status is not one of the fields available here. Full context and live catalog links are in the canonical analysis: https://househeating-pulse.com/guides/2026-eprel-heat-pump-inverter-share-in-europe

What the registry can support is structural analysis around the inverter question. The market is dominated by **air-water units (30,452 listings)** and **air-air units (21,065 listings)**, while **hp-water-heater** adds **9,228** more. That mix matters because any future inverter study will need to normalize by product type; otherwise, type composition will distort average efficiency and refrigerant comparisons.

Brand concentration is also high enough to matter. **Daikin Europe N.V.** accounts for **14,668 models** or **24.05%** of all listed products, followed by **Mitsubishi Electric Europe B.V.** at **5,575 models (9.14%)** and **Hitachi/Johnson Controls** at **5,207 (8.54%)**. Those suppliers will heavily shape any eventual inverter analysis, but the current corpus cannot say whether they rely more on inverter architectures than smaller manufacturers.

On performance, the snapshot’s overall **average SCOP is 4.55**, with notable variation by type: **ground-water** averages **4.77**, **air-water** **4.54**, and **water-water** **6.15**. Refrigerant mix is similarly skewed: **R32** leads with **13,935 listings**, while **R290** appears in **537**. Again, those are catalogue patterns, not inverter-specific evidence.
