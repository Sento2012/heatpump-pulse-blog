Europe’s 2026 heat-pump market is still structurally an **R32 market**, but the strategic signal is no longer about refrigerant chemistry alone. It is about which brands are using **R290 as a positioning tool** while the installed catalog still runs on scale and breadth. In EPREL, **13,935 models** are declared with R32, versus only **537 with R290** — about **22.85% vs 0.88%** of a **60,989-model** universe. That gap is too large to read propane as a mainstream default; it is still a niche with growing visibility.

The brand layer makes the split more interesting. The largest catalogs are still controlled by the incumbents: **Daikin Europe N.V.** lists **14,668 models** (**24.05%** of EPREL), followed by **Mitsubishi Electric Europe B.V.** at **5,575** and **Johnson Controls Hitachi Air Conditioning Europe** at **5,207**. Together, the top three account for **41.73%** of all listings. That concentration matters because any refrigerant pivot by a top-five manufacturer changes the market mix far more than the same move by a smaller specialist.

Efficiency does not map cleanly to scale. The market-wide average SCOP is **4.55**, while **Bosch Thermotechnik GmbH** posts **4.69**, **Ariston** **4.66**, and **Gree** **4.65**. By contrast, Daikin sits at **4.44** and Johnson Controls Hitachi at **4.18**. That does not prove a refrigerant effect, but it does show that catalog size and average performance are decoupled in 2026.
