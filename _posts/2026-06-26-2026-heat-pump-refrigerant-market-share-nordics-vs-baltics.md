R32 still defines the 2026 EPREL heat-pump market by volume, but the more important signal is how concentrated the catalogue remains around a few refrigerant codes and a few very large manufacturers. Across the full EU snapshot, 13,935 of 60,989 listed models declare R32, versus 1,896 for R410A and 537 for R290. That means natural refrigerants account for only 3.27% of listed models overall, even before any regional split is applied.

For the Nordics vs the Baltics, the key limitation is data availability: this corpus does **not** include a country-level basket for refrigerants, so a defensible Nordic/Baltic share comparison cannot be derived from the supplied EPREL extract. What the dataset does show is the structural backdrop behind that comparison. The catalogue is heavily weighted toward air-water systems, which make up 30,452 listings, or about 49.9% of the market, while air-air adds another 21,065 models. By contrast, ground-water and water-water remain niche categories at 213 and 31 listings respectively.

That matters because refrigerant choice is not the same as product positioning. A market with more cold-climate, higher-capacity, or niche hydronic systems can look different on paper even when refrigerant composition is similar. The overall market average SCOP is 4.55, and the type-level averages range from 4.54 for air-water to 6.15 for water-water, so system type still explains a lot of the efficiency spread.

Brand concentration is also high. Daikin Europe N.V. leads with 14,668 models and a 24.05% share, followed by Mitsubishi Electric Europe B.V. at 5,575 models and 9.14%. The top five brands together account for 51.93% of all listings, which implies that model availability is shaped more by portfolio depth than by any single refrigerant trend.
