Austria and Switzerland look similar on paper as Alpine heating markets, but the data only supports a narrow conclusion: **running-cost conditions favor heat pumps in Austria, while Switzerland has the cleaner grid**. Austria’s household electricity is listed at **€0.3272/kWh** against gas at **€0.1221/kWh**, giving an electricity-to-gas ratio of **2.68** — comfortably below the ~**3.7** break-even threshold used in the analysis. Switzerland’s tariff series is incomplete in the corpus, so no comparable ratio can be calculated there, even though its grid intensity is far lower at **28.0 gCO₂/kWh** versus **89.0 gCO₂/kWh** in Austria.

The broader EPREL market context matters because country-sliced product data is missing. Across the full indexed catalog, Househeating Pulse counts **60,989** listed heat-pump models from **777** manufacturers, with an average **SCOP of 4.55** and average declared power of **9.3 kW**. That universe is dominated by [air-to-water units](https://househeating-pulse.com/heat-pumps?type=air-water), which make up **30,452** listings, far ahead of air-to-air systems at **21,065**. So any Austria-vs-Switzerland claims about catalog breadth, brand concentration, or average efficiency would need country-filtered EPREL exports that are not present here.

What the registry does show is a concentrated vendor landscape: **Daikin Europe N.V.** accounts for **24.05%** of all indexed models, while **Mitsubishi Electric** and **Johnson Controls Hitachi** add **9.14%** and **8.54%** respectively. That top-three cluster alone represents **41.73%** of the catalog.

Efficiency and refrigerant mix also point to structural limits rather than national conclusions. The highest average SCOP in the database is **6.15** for water-to-water systems, but that category has only **31** models. Meanwhile, **R32** dominates with **13,935** listings, while **R290** remains a small minority at **540** propane-coded entries.
