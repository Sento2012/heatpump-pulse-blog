The main takeaway is a data limitation, not a leaderboard: the supplied EPREL extract does **not** contain country-filtered brand shares for Austria or Czechia, so no defensible claim can be made about which market is led by which brand. What it *does* show is the broader 2026 European baseline the two countries sit inside: **60,989 heat-pump models** from **777 manufacturers**, with a top-heavy brand structure and a product mix dominated by air-to-water systems.

At the Europe-wide level, [Daikin Europe N.V.](https://househeating-pulse.com/manufacturers/daikin-europe-n-v) is the clear volume leader with **14,668 listings** and a **24.05%** share. It is followed by [Mitsubishi Electric Europe B.V.](https://househeating-pulse.com/manufacturers/mitsubishi-electric-europe-b-v) at **5,575 models** and **9.14%**, then Johnson Controls Hitachi at **5,207** and **8.54%**. The top five are rounded out by Bosch Thermotechnik at **5.91%** and Ariston at **4.29%**, taking combined top-5 share to **51.93%**. That implies a concentrated catalogue even before any country-level filtering is applied.

Product mix is similarly clear. [Air-water heat pumps](https://househeating-pulse.com/heat-pumps?type=air-water) account for **30,452 listings**, about half the catalogue, while [air-air units](https://househeating-pulse.com/heat-pumps?type=air-air) add **21,065** more. On refrigerants, **R32** dominates with **13,935** declared entries, while **R290** is still relatively small at **537**. The market index estimates a **3.27%** natural-refrigerant share across the full listing universe, which is useful context for 2026 procurement and compliance screening.

Efficiency profiles also vary by manufacturer. Against the market-average SCOP of **4.55**, Bosch sits above the mean at **4.69** and Ariston at **4.66**, while Daikin’s average is **4.44** despite its scale advantage. That divergence matters for buyers comparing breadth of choice versus performance.
