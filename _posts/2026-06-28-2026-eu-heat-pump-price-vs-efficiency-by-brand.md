The key signal in the current EPREL snapshot is not a smooth trade-off between cost and performance; it is a wide **brand-level spread in efficiency** that makes any assumed “best value” shortcut unreliable. Across **60,989 models**, the market averages **4.55 SCOP**, but the top-10 brands by volume range from **4.18 SCOP** to **4.69 SCOP** — a gap large enough to change the outcome of a shortlist before price even enters the equation.

Volume leaders do not automatically sit near the top of the efficiency table. **Daikin Europe N.V.** holds **14,668 models** and **24.05%** of the catalog, yet averages **4.44 SCOP**, below the market mean. **Mitsubishi Electric Europe B.V.** is next at **5,575 models** and **4.51 SCOP**, also slightly under average. **Hitachi’s European entity** trails further at **4.18 SCOP**, which is materially weaker than both the market and its larger peers. By contrast, **Bosch Thermotechnik GmbH** reaches **4.69 SCOP** across **3,602 models**, while **Ariston SpA** posts **4.66 SCOP** across **2,618 models**.

That dispersion matters because the market is also structurally mixed: the snapshot includes **30,452 air-water systems**, **21,065 air-air units**, and **9,228 heat-pump water heaters**. In other words, the catalog is not dominated by one product class, so brand averages should be read alongside product mix, not in isolation. The same caution applies to refrigerants: **R32** still dominates with **13,935 models**, while **R290** remains relatively small at **537**.
