The key 2026 EPREL shift is not at the top, but in the runner-up slot: **Mitsubishi Electric Europe B.V. has moved ahead of Johnson Controls Hitachi to become No. 2 in Europe’s heat-pump model table**. Mitsubishi now lists **5,575 models**, versus **5,207** for Hitachi — a margin of **368 models** in a catalogue that totals **60,989 models from 777 manufacturers** across the EU dataset.

That matters because the gap is not just about volume. Mitsubishi’s average **SCOP is 4.51**, compared with **4.18** for Hitachi, so the brand that passed it is also posting a stronger seasonal-efficiency average. Both remain behind the market leader, **Daikin Europe N.V.**, which still dominates scale with **14,668 models** and **24.05%** share. But the ordering behind Daikin has changed in a way that signals who is consolidating breadth without losing too much efficiency.

For readers tracking market structure, Mitsubishi now represents **9.14%** of all listed models, slightly ahead of Hitachi’s **8.54%**. That puts the two brands together at **17.68%** of the EPREL market. The next brand down, Bosch Thermotechnik GmbH, sits well behind at **3,602 models**, so the second place position is not a narrow statistical artifact; it is a real separation in catalogue depth.

The efficiency context is also useful. The overall EU average SCOP is **4.55**. Mitsubishi sits just **0.04 below** that benchmark, while Hitachi is **0.37 below**. Daikin’s average is **4.44**, so Mitsubishi is also slightly ahead of the leader on this metric. That combination — near-market-average efficiency plus large-scale breadth — is why the move is worth watching.
