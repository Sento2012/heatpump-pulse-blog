R290 has crossed the point where it can be tracked as a market segment rather than a launch curiosity. EPREL now lists **537 R290 heat-pump models**, which is **0.88%** of the **60,989-model** European database. That is still small next to **R32 at 13,935 listings**, but the key shift is that propane is now visible at scale: enough volume to measure brand strategy, not just count announcements.

The wider refrigerant picture still shows a concentrated transition. All natural refrigerants combined account for **3.27%** of listings, so R290 already makes up most of that pool. In other words, the propane category is carrying the natural-refrigerant story in EPREL, even if it has not displaced the incumbent refrigerants in overall volume.

The brand question is where the data gets more structural. The full EPREL market is dominated by a small number of large manufacturers: **Daikin Europe N.V.** leads with **14,668 listings** (**24.05%** share), followed by **Mitsubishi Electric Europe B.V.** at **5,575** and **9.14%**, and **Johnson Controls Hitachi** at **5,207** and **8.54%**. The top 10 brands control **60.89%** of all listings. That concentration matters because new refrigerant adoption rarely spreads evenly across hundreds of vendors at once.

This is why the 2026 signal is not “R290 is everywhere.” It is that R290 has reached a level where the next question is no longer whether it exists, but which incumbents are converting meaningful parts of their catalog. The strongest read is that early propane adoption is likely to come from the same heavyweight manufacturers that already dominate EPREL, not from a flat, long-tail rollout.
