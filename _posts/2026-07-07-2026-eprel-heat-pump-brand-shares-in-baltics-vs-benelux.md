The main finding is methodological: the snapshot does **not** support a true Baltics-vs-Benelux brand ranking, because the corpus lacks country-group splits for Lithuania, Latvia, Estonia, Belgium, the Netherlands, and Luxembourg. What it *does* show is how concentrated the broader EPREL heat-pump catalog is, and that context matters for any regional read-through.

At the EU-wide level, **Daikin Europe N.V.** leads with **14,668 models** and a **24.05%** share. The next two brands are well behind: **Mitsubishi Electric Europe B.V.** at **5,575 models** (**9.14%**) and **Johnson Controls Hitachi** at **5,207** (**8.54%**). Together, the top three account for **37.73%** of all listed models, and the top five reach **51.93%** once **Bosch Thermotechnik** (**5.91%**) and **Ariston** (**4.29%**) are included. The live leaderboard logic is available in the [EPREL heat-pump catalog](https://househeating-pulse.com/guides/2026-eprel-heat-pump-brand-shares-in-baltics-vs-benelux) and the broader [manufacturer index](https://househeating-pulse.com/manufacturers).

Efficiency is less tightly clustered than volume. Among the top five brands, average SCOP ranges from **4.18** for Johnson Controls Hitachi to **4.69** for Bosch, with Ariston close behind at **4.66** and Daikin at **4.44**. That gap is small in absolute terms, but enough to show why model-count leadership does not automatically imply the strongest efficiency profile.

The type mix is also dominated by a few categories: **air-water** units make up **30,452 models** or roughly **49.93%** of the catalog, while **air-air** sits at **21,065** models and **34.54%**. Smaller segments are structurally limited, with only **31 water-water** models in the snapshot.

R290 vs R32, and any Baltics-vs-Benelux refrigerant split, cannot be quantified from this extract.
