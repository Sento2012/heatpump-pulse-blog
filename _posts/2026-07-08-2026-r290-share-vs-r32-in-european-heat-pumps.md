# R290 is gaining strategic relevance, but R32 still owns the 2026 volume base

The key shift in Europe’s 2026 heat-pump catalog is not that propane has become dominant — it hasn’t — but that it is now visible enough to matter in portfolio strategy. EPREL lists **537 R290 models** versus **13,935 R32 models**, so propane still sits at just **0.88%** of listed products, while R32 holds **22.85%**. That is a gap of **21.97 percentage points**, and it keeps R32 firmly in charge of declared volume.

The more important read is structural. Out of **60,989** total models from **777 manufacturers**, R290 has moved beyond niche status and now ranks **third** in declared refrigerant usage, behind only R32 and R410A. In absolute terms, R32 still has about **25.95 times** as many listings as R290, but the market signal is no longer trivial: a few hundred propane models can now influence compliance planning, sales messaging, and installer training across a catalog this large.

Regulation is part of why the split matters. R32 carries a **771 GWP** and a listed phase-out date of **2027-01-01**, while R290 has **GWP 0** and no phase-out date in the reference table. That makes the refrigerant choice less about near-term volume and more about how manufacturers position themselves for the post-F-gas transition.

The concentration of the European market means small refrigerant shifts can still move the narrative. **Daikin Europe** alone represents **24.05%** of listed models, and the top five brands together account for **51.93%**. Even without brand-by-brand refrigerant splits, that concentration means decisions by a handful of manufacturers will shape whether R290 remains a signal or becomes a mainstream default.
