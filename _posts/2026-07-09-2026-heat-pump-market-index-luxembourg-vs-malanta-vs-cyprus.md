## Luxembourg, Malta and Cyprus do not compare on “market maturity” the way a product catalog would suggest

The decisive difference in this three-country comparison is not the missing catalog data — it is the operating environment. Househeating Pulse’s 2026 index shows that Luxembourg, Malta and Cyprus cannot be ranked by country-level model count, average price, SCOP, refrigerant mix or brand concentration from the available EPREL-style corpus. What can be ranked is demand structure, electricity cost and carbon intensity, and those variables point to three very different heat-pump markets.

On cost, Malta is the most favorable setup: household electricity is **EUR 0.1282/kWh**, versus **EUR 0.2665/kWh** in Luxembourg and **EUR 0.2774/kWh** in Cyprus. That means Cyprus is about **2.16×** Malta on electricity price alone, with Luxembourg also more than double Malta. On climate, Luxembourg is the only heating-heavy market in the group, with **3,287.76 heating degree days**, compared with **492.27** in Malta and **819.26** in Cyprus. The island markets are the reverse: cooling demand is much higher, at **1,039.03** cooling degree days in Malta and **1,570.64** in Cyprus, versus only **136.92** in Luxembourg.

Carbon intensity splits the group again. Luxembourg’s grid is far cleaner at **53.0 gCO₂/kWh**, while Malta is **388.0 gCO₂/kWh** and Cyprus reaches **543.0 gCO₂/kWh**. So if the question is lifecycle emissions, Luxembourg is the strongest case for electrified heating; if the question is operating cost, Malta wins; if the question is summer-weighted demand, Cyprus stands out.

For a broader benchmark, the European snapshot behind the index includes **60,989 heat-pump models** from **777 manufacturers**, with average SCOP at **4.55** and R32 still the dominant declared refrigerant across the continent.
