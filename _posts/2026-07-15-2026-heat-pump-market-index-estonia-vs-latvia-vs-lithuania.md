## 2026 Baltic heat-pump index: the market signal is energy context, not local catalog size

The sharpest finding is what the registry **cannot** prove: it does not support a country-by-country claim that Estonia, Latvia, or Lithuania has the deepest EPREL catalog, the lowest listing prices, or the highest average efficiency. What it **does** show is that Baltic buyers are selecting from a very large European supply base: **60,989 heat-pump models** from **777 manufacturers**, with an overall average **SCOP of 4.55**, average capacity of **9.3 kW**, and average outdoor sound power of **61.3 dB** in the latest Househeating Pulse snapshot.

That matters because the Baltic operating environment is similar but not identical. All three countries are tagged as **“colder”** climate-zone markets, yet the economic backdrop differs enough to change payback math. Lithuania has the lowest household electricity price at **€0.1955/kWh** and the most favorable electricity-to-gas ratio at **2.86**. Latvia follows at **€0.2452/kWh** and **2.97**, while Estonia is highest on the ratio side at **3.03** and also has the coldest profile among the three at **4,474.47 heating degree days**.

At the product level, the European catalog is still dominated by mainstream **air-water** systems: **30,452 models** with average **SCOP 4.54**. More specialized niches, such as **water-water** units, reach much higher average efficiency (**SCOP 6.15**) and lower noise (**42.0 dB**), but with only **31 models** they are not the core residential choice set. Refrigerants show a similar concentration: **R32** appears on **13,935 listings**, far ahead of **R290** at **537**.

The practical takeaway is simple: for the Baltics, efficiency and refrigerant choice matter more than a mythical “best country” catalog ranking.
