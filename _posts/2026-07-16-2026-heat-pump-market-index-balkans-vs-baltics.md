# 2026 Heat-Pump Market Index: the Balkans vs the Baltics

The clearest signal in the data is not brand breadth or average product price — it is household economics. On the tariff side, the **Baltics** come out slightly ahead on electricity-to-gas pricing, with an average ratio of **2.95** across Lithuania, Latvia, and Estonia, versus **3.09** for the Balkan countries represented in the corpus. That gap is small, but it matters because both regions sit below the rough **3.7** break-even line where heat pumps tend to compete more cleanly with gas on operating cost.

Country detail shows the spread inside the two baskets. In the Baltics, Lithuania is at **2.86**, Latvia at **2.97**, and Estonia at **3.03**. In the Balkan set, Bulgaria is stronger at **2.09**, Slovenia at **2.44**, and Greece at **2.59**, but the basket is pulled up by Croatia at **3.05**, Hungary at **3.23**, and especially Romania at **5.11**. That makes the Balkan picture less uniform, even if parts of it still look favorable for electrification.

The broader EPREL market context is also heavily concentrated. [Daikin Europe N.V.](https://househeating-pulse.com/manufacturers/daikin-europe-n-v) alone accounts for **14,668 models**, or **24.05%** of all listings. Mitsubishi Electric follows at **5,575** models (**9.14%**), while Johnson Controls Hitachi holds **5,207** (**8.54%**). For regional interpretation, that concentration matters: a small market can look fragmented simply because the long tail is visible, not because the leaders are absent.

Refrigerants show the transition underway but not yet complete. Across EPREL, **R32** appears on **13,935** listings, while exact-code **R290** appears on **537**. The full analysis also explains why the data does **not** support claiming either region is already “R290-led,” despite the policy and product shift toward lower-GWP options.
