The sharpest result is not a ranking, but a data gap: the supplied 2026 EPREL corpus does **not** contain a Baltic-region or Balkan-region slice, so it cannot support a defensible comparison of brand mix, median price, SCOP, or refrigerant share between those two markets. What it *does* establish is the European baseline the regional analysis would have to beat: **60,989 models** from **777 manufacturers**, with listing concentration dominated by a small set of brands.

At the Europe-wide level, **Daikin** leads with **24.05%** of listings, followed by **Mitsubishi Electric** at **9.14%** and **Hitachi** at **8.54%**. The top five brands together account for **51.93%** of all listed models, which is a strong reminder that “market breadth” is not the same as “market depth.” For efficiency, **Bosch Thermotechnik** stands out in the top five with an average **SCOP of 4.69**, ahead of **Ariston** at **4.66**, while Hitachi trails at **4.18**.

Type mix is equally important. Across Europe, **air-water** dominates with **30,452 models**, while **air-air** follows at **21,065**. On performance, the niche **water-water** category posts the highest average SCOP at **6.15**, but on just **31 listings** — a useful illustration of why raw efficiency averages need type normalization before anyone compares regions.

Refrigerants point to the same structural issue. **R32** appears in **13,935 listings**, while **R290** is present in only **537**, and the market-wide natural refrigerant share is just **3.27%**. That makes any Baltic-vs-Balkan F-gas exposure analysis highly relevant — and impossible here without a regional extract.
