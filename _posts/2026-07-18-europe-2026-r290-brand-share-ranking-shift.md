The clearest signal in the 2026 EPREL data is not a dramatic R290 breakthrough, but how small the segment still is relative to the broader heat-pump market. Only **537 R290 listings** are visible, equal to **3.27%** of the indexed market, so even a modest wave of registrations can move the ranking materially. That makes propane-based leadership more fragile — and more worth tracking — than the headline totals suggest.

At the same time, the overall market remains highly concentrated. **Daikin Europe N.V.** still leads with **14,668 listings**, followed by **Mitsubishi Electric Europe B.V.** at **5,575** and **Johnson Controls Hitachi** at **5,207**. Together, the top three account for **41.73%** of all indexed listings, which is a useful benchmark for judging how much room smaller brands have to close gaps in niche segments. In other words: if R290 leadership is changing, it is happening inside a market that still rewards scale.

The efficiency picture is also mixed. Volume leadership does not automatically line up with the strongest seasonal performance. **Bosch Thermotechnik GmbH** posts the highest average SCOP among the top 10 at **4.69**, ahead of **Ariston** at **4.66** and **Vaillant** at **4.54**, while Daikin sits at **4.44**. That matters because R290 adoption is often discussed as both a compliance and performance story, not just a portfolio-size story.

The catch: the current corpus does not include a clean R290-only manufacturer table or an earlier comparable snapshot, so exact position changes within the propane segment cannot be verified here. The broader implication is still clear — R290 is a small, volatile category layered on top of a market where a few manufacturers dominate visibility.
